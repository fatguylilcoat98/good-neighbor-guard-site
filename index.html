Example question
"Someone called saying I owe the IRS money and I'll be arrested if I don't pay right now with gift cards. Is this real?"
This is a scam. The IRS never calls demanding immediate payment by gift card or threatens arrest. Real IRS notices always come by mail first. You do not owe anything from this call.
What you should do right now:

1. Do not call back and do not send any money.
2. Report it to the IRS at irs.gov/phishing or call 1-800-366-4484.
3. Report to FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov.
4. Tell family members — this same call targets many people.

The IRS will always send written notice before any contact and will never demand gift cards or instant wire transfers.
